Solution in Feres, homework 8.

The problem is the following: we draw independent exponential random variables \( T_1, T_2, \ldots \) of parameter \( \lambda \). We do this \( N \) times, where \( N \) is an independent geometric random variable of parameter \( \beta \).

The hypothesis is: the stopping time \( T = T_1 + T_2 + \ldots + T_N \) after these draws has exponential distribution of parameter \( \lambda \beta \).

This can be proven analytically (see the notes of Feres) and can also be made visible by simulations. Here, we see that for \( \lambda=1.0 \), the mean stopping time is just \( 1/\beta \). This is exactly the mean of the exponential distribution with parameter \( \beta \).
