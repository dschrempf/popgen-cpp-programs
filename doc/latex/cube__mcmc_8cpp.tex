\subsection{Detailed Description}
Markov Chains -\/ Norris; exercise 1.\+7.\+3.; simulation of a Markov chain that moves around the eight vertices of a cube. 

\begin{DoxyAuthor}{Author}
Dominik Schrempf \href{mailto:dominik.schrempf@gmail.com}{\tt dominik.\+schrempf@gmail.\+com} 
\end{DoxyAuthor}
\begin{DoxyDate}{Date}
Wed Feb 11 18\+:33\+:21 2015
\end{DoxyDate}
Solution in Feres, homework 6.

Simulate a Markov chain that moves around the eight vertices of a cube. Each step, the chain is equally likely to move to each of the three adjacent vertices, independently of its past move. Let i be the initial vertex occupied by the chain, o the vertex opposite i\+:



The following quantities are calculated\+:


\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item the expected number of steps until the particle returns to i;
\item the expected number of visits to o until the first return to i;
\item the expected number of steps until the first visit to o. 
\end{DoxyEnumerate}
